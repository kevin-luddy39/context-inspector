## Long version (LinkedIn About / website)

With over three decades of experience in software design and architecture, my focus remains on developing scalable, feature-rich systems that service business needs. The infusion of AI into the public consciousness and workplace opens up opportunities to use it in ever more clever ways. My experience with AI-fueled development and agentic system design and implementation continues to expand. The core competencies I have spent 37 years applying my craft to only become enhanced as my repertoire of AI tool use keeps pace with developing technologies.

Recent work sits squarely at that intersection. I've been building full-stack AI systems end-to-end: a **job-search automation platform** (Python/Flask) that polls Gmail, classifies recruiter email with an LLM, extracts structured job records, tailors resumes against specific job descriptions via the Anthropic API, and generates ATS-optimized PDFs — with usage telemetry and a SQLite-backed tracker underneath. In parallel, I built **Context Inspector**, an open-source MCP server and statistical diagnostic suite (Node.js) that monitors the bell-curve health of AI context windows in real time and detects domain-alignment degradation before output quality fails — the tool-form of a white paper I authored on early-warning signals for AI system decay.

Across both projects, the through-line is the same discipline I've applied for 37 years: clean service boundaries, observable behavior, pragmatic data models, and architectures that hold up when the requirements shift. AI is the newest tool in the kit — not a replacement for the kit.

**Core competencies:** software architecture, full-stack engineering (Python/Flask, Node.js), LLM integration (Anthropic, MCP), agentic/multi-agent system design, statistical analysis and NLP diagnostics, API design, email/document pipelines (Gmail, DOCX, PDF), SQLite/embedded data, prompt engineering, and AI-augmented development workflows.
